INDORI

Indore's food culture blends Malwi, Maharashtrian, Gujarati, and Rajasthani influences.

The enticing part of Indore is the popular street food culture. The two famous food joints are Chappan Dukan and Sarafa Bazaar.

Chappan Dukan's development as a Smart food street is a part of the smart city project worth INR 40 million and was implemented in 45 days.

Sarafa Bazaar, which is Indore's only night food market. Attracts massive crowds from across the city and tourists from other places. Indore is also notably famous for various savorys and namkeens stores across the city. The most popular food recipes include - Poha, samosa, kachori, jalebi, rabdi, gulab jamun, gajak, bhel, imarti, pani-puri, egg banjo, moong dal bhujiya, moong dal halwa, sabudhana wada and khichdi, dahi wada, dhokla, sev, and jeeravan.

The city is most likely the pure vegetarian city in India. 49% of the population are pure vegetarians.
Indore serves as the headquarters of both the Indore district and the Indore Division. It is well-found to be the cleanest city. It is also the educational hub of Madhya Pradesh, housing the campuses of the Indian Institute of Management and the Indian Institute of Technology. It is located at Malwa Plateau, the southern edge of Indore. With an altitude of 553 meters above sea level, it is the highest elevation among the cities of central India.

The city is spread across 530 sq kilometers (200 sq miles), with current population of 3,302,000, a 2.9% increase from 2022 with the metro population of 3,209,000, a 3.08% increase from 2021.

Indore is Madhya Pradesh state's financial capital and has been home to the Madhya Pradesh Stock since 2015. It has been chosen as one of the 100 Indian cities to have developed as a smart city.

It has been a part of the Swachh Surveshan since its start and ranked 25th in 2016. Indore has been chosen as the cleanest city for six consecutive years. 2017 to 2022, and it is the first water-plus city under the Swachh Surakshnan Ayojan 2021.

Indore is the only city chosen for the International Clean Air Catalyst Programme. The project, in collaboration with the Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board and Indore Municipal Corporation, is scheduled to operate for five years to purify the air in the city.
